About This Initiative

What Is This System?

The Digital Animal Carcass Management System is a government-backed environmental initiative that empowers citizens to report, locate, and coordinate the hygienic disposal of animal carcasses. Improper disposal leads to the spread of disease, pollution of water bodies, and ecological damage. Our platform bridges the gap between citizens and municipal agencies — making the process fast, transparent, and eco-friendly.

The Problem

Dead Animals — A Silent Environmental Crisis

Thousands of animal carcasses are left unattended every year, creating serious public health hazards:

  • Spread of dangerous pathogens and zoonotic diseases
  • Contamination of groundwater and rivers
  • Foul odours and attraction of scavengers
  • Soil degradation and loss of biodiversity
  • Lack of awareness about proper reporting channels

Why It Matters

Environmental Impact

Understanding the consequences drives better action. Here's how improper disposal affects us.

Water Contamination

Decomposing carcasses leach pathogens and nitrates into soil and water sources, rendering them unfit for use.

High Risk
Disease Spread

Rotting carcasses become breeding grounds for bacteria and insects that transmit diseases to humans and livestock.

Critical Concern
Organic Opportunity

Managed composting of animal carcasses produces nutrient-rich organic fertilizer for sustainable agriculture.
